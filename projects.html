Projects

I am currently working in two areas: (1) Cultural Analytics, or the use of computational, AI, and data science methods to study and analyze culture, particularly contemporary digital and online culture, and (2) Cultural AI—the experimental testing of generative AI models to perform and enact cultural tasks and behavior, such as authorship and aesthetic interpretation, as a means to improving AI systems from the perspective of cultural insight and theory.

Cultural Analytics

  • Fast Culture, Slow Justice: Storytelling and Social Movements in the Digital Age (book in progress) — A case study of #BLM in 2020 that explores how platforms like Twitter and Wattpad transformed public storytelling and their role in galvanizing social movements.
  • Split Screens: Aesthetic Preference and Political Polarization (with Tess McNulty and Hoyt Long, under review) — A study of the relationship between TV aesthetic preference/viewing and US political voting from 2016–2022, with a focus on polarization.
  • Cultural and Aesthetic Sources for Political and Social Behavior (with David Bamman, Hoyt Long, and Tess McNulty) — A series of papers (2016–present) combining machine vision analysis of popular TV shows with social/political data to understand how aesthetic and narrative preferences relate to political behavior.

Cultural AI

  • The Social AI Author (with Edwin Roland and Hoyt Long) — Generative AI simulations designed to explore how AI agents behave as authors and how they model social preferences and creative success.
  • Close Reading and LLM Co-intelligence (with Mina Lee, Jiayin Zhi, and Hoyt Long) — Human-computer interaction experiments testing whether LLMs can enhance students' ability to close read and enjoy poetry.
  • Cultural AI: Building a New Research Field (with Aarthi Vadde) — A methodological paper outlining the goals of a new research area focused on enriching AI with humanistic insight and developing tools for cultural research.
